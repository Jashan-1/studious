# Multiple Choice Questions

## Question 1
What is the main theme of Anton Chekhov’s play "The Proposal"?

A. The complexities of true love  
B. The tendency of wealthy families to arrange marriages for economic gain  
C. The cultural differences between Russian and Indian weddings  
D. The importance of friendship in marriage

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** The play centers on wealthy families encouraging marriages that make economic sense, not necessarily those based on love.

## Question 2
Which of the following best describes the custom of “the bride being stolen” during a Russian wedding reception?

A. The bride runs away to test the groom's devotion  
B. The bride's friends hide her, and the groom must pay a fee to get her back  
C. The bride is taken to another city for a second ceremony  
D. The groom’s family takes the bride to their home immediately after the reception

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** The bride is hidden by her friends, and the groom is required to pay a fee for her return, which is a playful tradition at Russian weddings.

## Question 3
