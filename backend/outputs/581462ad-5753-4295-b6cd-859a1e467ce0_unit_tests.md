## Question 1
What are some key differences in the planning and customs of Russian weddings compared to Indian weddings as mentioned in the content?

**Model Answer:**
The planning of Russian weddings is simpler, typically involving arrangements for rings, the bride's dress, cars, and a reception. In contrast, Indian weddings often include more elaborate traditions and rituals. Additionally, Russian weddings can last for several days and involve customs like the bride being 'stolen,' whereas Indian weddings may have different cultural practices and ceremonies that vary widely across regions.

**Key Points:**
- Simplicity of Russian wedding preparations versus complexity of Indian weddings
- Duration of weddings in both cultures
- Unique customs like the bride being 'stolen' in Russian weddings

## Question 2
How do Lomov's and Natalya's arguments about Oxen Meadows reflect their characters and the theme of the play?

**Model Answer:**
Lomov's and Natalya's arguments about Oxen Meadows illustrate their quarrelsome natures and the theme of wealth and property in relationships. Their inability to agree on a trivial matter highlights their stubbornness and the superficiality of their motives for marriage, as they prioritize land ownership over the significance of their potential union.

**Key Points:**
- The quarrelsome nature of both characters
- Reflection of the theme of wealth and property
- Superficial reasons for marriage overshadowing genuine connection

## Question 3
What does the tradition of the groom having to answer questions before reaching the bride signify in Russian wedding customs?

**Model Answer:**
The tradition of the groom answering questions posed by the bride's friends signifies a test of his commitment and ability to prove himself worthy. This playful challenge adds an element of fun to the ceremony while also emphasizing the importance of friendship and support from the bride's community in the wedding process.

**Key Points:**
- Test of commitment and worthiness
- Element of fun in wedding customs
- Importance of community and friendship in the ceremony

## Question 4
Discuss the significance of toasts during the reception in a Russian wedding.

**Model Answer:**
Toasts during the reception are significant as they serve to honor and celebrate the newly-married couple. This tradition fosters a sense of community and shared joy among guests, while also reinforcing social bonds. The act of raising glasses together symbolizes unity and the collective support of family and friends for the couple's future.

**Key Points:**
- Celebration of the couple through toasts
- Fostering community and social bonds
- Symbolism of unity and support among guests

## Question 5
In what ways do the economic motivations behind marriages in "The Proposal" reflect the societal values of the time?

**Model Answer:**
The economic motivations behind marriages in "The Proposal" reflect a societal value system that prioritizes wealth and property over romantic love. Characters like Lomov and Chubukov represent the upper class, where marrying for financial stability is seen as a practical necessity. This focus on economic gain rather than emotional connection reveals the social pressures and expectations placed on individuals within wealthy families during Chekhov's time.

**Key Points:**
- Marriage as a means to secure wealth and property
- Reflection of upper-class societal values
- Contrast between economic necessity and romantic ideals
