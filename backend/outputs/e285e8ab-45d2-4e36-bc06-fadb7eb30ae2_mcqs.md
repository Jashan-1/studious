# Multiple Choice Questions

## Question 1
What did Kisa Gotami initially seek from her neighbors after her son's death?

A. Money to arrange a funeral  
B. Medicine to bring her son back to life  
C. Advice on coping with grief  
D. Help to find the Buddha  

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Kisa Gotami asked her neighbors for medicine to cure her dead son, not realizing yet that he could not be brought back to life.

## Question 2
What condition did the Buddha give Kisa Gotami for obtaining the mustard-seed?

A. The seed must be holy  
B. It must be from a peepal tree  
C. It must come from a house where no one had died  
D. It must be blessed by a monk  

**Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** The Buddha asked for mustard-seed from a house where no one had lost a loved one, helping Kisa realize the universality of death.

---

## Question 3
After visiting many houses, what important realization did Kisa Gotami come to?

A. That mustard-seeds were very rare  
B. That grief is unique to her  
C. That death is universal and affects every family  
D. That she should become a monk  

**Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** Kisa Gotami realized every household had experienced loss, teaching her that suffering from death is universal.

## Question 4
What is the main message of the Buddha’s sermon at Benares as recounted in the text?

A. All suffering can be avoided through prayer  
B. Only the wise can escape death  
C. Death and suffering are universal, and peace comes from overcoming selfish grief  
D. The dead can be revived with enough effort  

**Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** The sermon emphasizes the inevitability of death and the importance of accepting it to achieve peace of mind.

## Question 5
In the context of grief, which of the following feelings is NOT mentioned in the "Guide to Coping with the Death of a Loved One"?

A. Sadness  
B. Excitement  
C. Guilt  
D. Anger  

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Sadness, guilt, and anger are discussed as common feelings during grief, while excitement is not.

## Question 6
What does the phrase “valley of desolation” most closely mean as used in the text?

A. A fertile area full of life  
B. A place of deep sorrow and loss  
C. A mountain of hope  
D. A river of wisdom  

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** "Valley of desolation" refers to a metaphorical place of great sadness and grief.

## Question 7
Why did the Buddha use the example of ripe fruits falling or earthen vessels breaking in his sermon?

A. To illustrate the randomness of happiness  
B. To show the beauty of nature  
C. To demonstrate that all living beings are subject to death  
D. To encourage people to farm and make pottery  

**Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** The examples were meant to show the certainty and universality of death for all living beings.

## Question 8
In "Good Grief" by Amitai Etzioni, what advice provided comfort to the grieving family?

A. To follow the stages of grief in order  
B. To focus on what survivors can do for one another  
C. To ignore their feelings  
D. To move to a new city  

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** The advice “We are not to ask why, but what,” led the bereaved to focus on supporting each other as a source of consolation.

## Question 9

A. Memorize religious texts  
B. Understand different meanings and connotations of the word  
C. Learn to write a sermon

## Question 10
How does Kahlil Gibran’s view of joy and sorrow, as quoted in the text, relate to the Buddha’s teachings?

A. Sorrow and joy are unrelated  
B. Grief should be hidden  
C. Both view joy and sorrow as intertwined and inseparable parts of human experience  
D. Only sorrow should be acknowledged  

**Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** Both Gibran and the Buddha convey that joy and sorrow are deeply connected and that understanding one helps appreciate the other.
