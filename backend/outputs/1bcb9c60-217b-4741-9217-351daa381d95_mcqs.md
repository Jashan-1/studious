# Multiple Choice Questions

## Question 1
What was the main lesson the Buddha intended Kisa Gotami to learn through her quest for mustard seeds?

A. That she needed to find a rare medicine to cure her son  
B. That suffering and death are universal experiences  
C. That the Buddha could perform miracles  
D. That she should continue to grieve for her son  

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** The Buddha wanted Kisa Gotami to realize that death and suffering are common to all, and that she was not alone in her grief.

A. Blessed by

## Question 3
Why did Kisa Gotami fail to obtain the mustard seeds as the Buddha had requested?

A. No one had mustard seeds to give her  
B. Every house had experienced a loss, so none met the Buddha’s condition  
C. The mustard seeds were too expensive  
D. She gave up too soon  

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** She could not find any house that had not lost a loved one, demonstrating that suffering and death are universal.

## Question 4
Which of the following best describes the Buddha’s advice on how to achieve peace of mind?

A. By ignoring grief and pretending it doesn't exist  
B. By drawing out the arrow of lamentation, complaint, and grief  
C. By seeking revenge for one’s suffering  
D. By isolating oneself from others  

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** The Buddha taught that removing grief and lamentation from one’s heart leads to peace of mind.

## Question 5
In Amitai Etzioni’s reflection, what is suggested as the most consoling response to grief?

A. Focusing on the past and what has been lost  
B. Strictly following prescribed stages of mourning  
C. Engaging in supportive actions for each other  
D. Suppressing all emotions  

**Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** Etzioni found consolation in focusing on what survivors do for each other, rather than asking “why.”

## Question 6
According to the text, what does the phrase “the living are few, but the dead are many” signify in the context of Kisa Gotami’s quest?

A. That death is rare and shocking  
B. That most people are alive and unaffected by grief  
C. That death is inevitable and has touched every family  
D. That Kisa Gotami was looking in the wrong place  

**Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** The phrase highlights the universality of death and loss experienced by all families.

## Question 8
How does Kahlil Gibran, in “Joy and Sorrow,” describe the relationship between joy and sorrow?

A. Joy and sorrow are completely separate and unrelated  
B. Joy is always greater than sorrow  
C. Joy and sorrow are inseparable and arise from the same source  
D. Sorrow should be avoided to experience joy  

**Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** Gibran states that joy and sorrow come together and are part of the same experience.

## Question 9
What does the poem “For Anne Gregory” suggest about the nature of love?

A. People can only be loved for their physical appearance  
B. Only God can love a person for themselves alone, not for their appearance  
C. Changing one’s appearance guarantees true love  
D. Love is always based on intelligence  

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** The poem concludes that only God can love someone for who they are, not for their external qualities.

## Question 10
Why did Kisa Gotami come to see her own grief as ‘selfish’ by the end of her journey?

A. She realized she had caused others pain  
B. She recognized that she wanted to escape suffering that everyone must face  
C. She no longer cared about her son  
D. She was angry at the Buddha  

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Kisa Gotami saw her grief as selfish because she initially wanted to avoid a universal experience, seeking a solution for herself alone.
