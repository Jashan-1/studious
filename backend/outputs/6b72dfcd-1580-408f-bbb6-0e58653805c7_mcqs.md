# Multiple Choice Questions

## Question 1
What did Kisa Gotami initially seek from her neighbors after her son died?

A. Money to perform a funeral  
B. Medicine to cure her son  
C. Advice on dealing with grief  
D. Food for her family  

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Kisa Gotami sought medicine to cure her dead son, hoping to bring him back to life, which shows her initial denial of his death.

---

## Question 2
Why did Kisa Gotami fail to get the mustard seeds as requested by the Buddha?

A. No one had mustard seeds  
B. Every house had experienced a loss  
C. People refused to help her  
D. She did not search enough houses  

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** The Buddha asked for mustard seeds from a house where no one had died; Kisa Gotami discovered that every house had suffered a loss, illustrating the universality of death.

---

## Question 3
What realization did Kisa Gotami come to after visiting many houses?

A. That she needed more faith  
B. That sorrow is unique to her  
C. That death is common to all  
D. That she should blame the Buddha  

**Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** Kisa Gotami realized that death is a universal experience, not unique to her, which was the deeper lesson the Buddha intended.

## Question 4
According to the Buddha’s sermon, what is the wise person’s response to grief?

A. To lament and weep  
B. To seek revenge  
C. To accept death and not grieve  
D. To isolate themselves  

**Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** The Buddha teaches that the wise accept the reality of death and do not grieve, understanding that suffering and death are inevitable.

---

## Question 5
How does the Buddha use the metaphor of ripe fruits and earthen vessels in his sermon?

A. To describe the beauty of life  
B. To show the fragility and inevitability of death  
C. To encourage people to plant trees  
D. To explain how to live longer  

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** The Buddha compares humans to ripe fruits (which fall) and earthen vessels (which break), illustrating that all living beings are bound to die.

## Question 7
In the context of the poem "For Anne Gregory," what does the phrase “great honey-coloured ramparts at your ear” refer to?

A. The woman’s jewelry  
B. The woman’s hair  
C. The castle walls  
D. The woman’s earrings  

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** The phrase refers to the young woman's beautiful, yellow-blonde hair, which the young man suggests is the reason for others’ admiration.

## Question 8
Which feeling is NOT commonly listed as a part of the grieving process, according to the guide on coping with grief?

A. Sadness  
B. Excitement

D. Guilt

## Question 9
What advice did Amitai Etzioni’s relative give to his grieving family?

A. Focus on asking "why" the loss occurred  
B. Ignore feelings and focus on work only  
C. Focus on "what" survivors can do for each other  
D. Seek professional therapy immediately  

**Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** The relative advised not to dwell on “why” but instead to focus on “what” survivors can do for each other, emphasizing action and mutual support.

## Question 10
Which of the following best expresses the message in Tagore’s lines shared in the content?

A. Death should be feared and mourned  
B. We should be thankful for the life that was lived  
C. The dawn is always sadder than the night  
D. Grief is more important than joy  

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Tagore’s lines encourage gratitude for the life of the departed, seeing death not as an end but a transition, like the lamp being put out because dawn has come.
